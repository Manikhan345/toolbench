Questions

Does compressing reduce image quality?

At quality 80 the difference is invisible to the eye on most photos, while the file drops 60–80%. Lower the slider and you'll start to see softening in fine detail and gradients. Photos tolerate compression well; screenshots and flat graphics do better as PNG or at higher quality.

Which format should I pick?

WebP for almost everything on the web — it's supported by every current browser and produces the smallest file. JPG if you need maximum compatibility with older software. PNG only when you need transparency or pixel-exact flat graphics.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. Compression happens entirely inside your browser tab. Nothing is transmitted, stored, or logged.

Why does my PNG get bigger as a PNG?

Canvas re-encodes PNG without the original's optimisation, so a heavily optimised PNG can grow. Convert it to WebP instead — that's where the real saving is.

Is there a file size limit?

No hard limit, but very large images (over ~30MP) can be slow on phones since the work happens on your device.
