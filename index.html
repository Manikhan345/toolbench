Questions

Compress or convert — which do I want?

Compress keeps the file type you already have and just shrinks it. Convert changes the file type, which is usually the bigger win: the same photo as WebP is typically 25–35% smaller than JPG at matching quality, and AVIF smaller still.

Does compressing hurt quality?

At quality 80 the difference is invisible on most photos while the file drops 60–80%. Use the compare slider on any result to judge it yourself. Photos handle compression well; flat graphics and screenshots do better as PNG or at higher quality.

Are my images sent to a server?

No. Everything runs inside your browser tab. Nothing is transmitted, stored, or logged, and there is no account to create.

Why did my PNG get bigger?

Re-encoding a PNG discards the original file's optimisation, so an already-optimised PNG can grow. Convert it to WebP or AVIF instead. The tool flags this when it happens.

Is there a limit on file size or count?

No imposed limit. Very large images can be slow on phones because the work happens on your own device.
