Questions

Does compressing reduce image quality?

At quality 80 the difference is invisible to the eye on most photos, while the file drops 60–80%. Use the compare slider on any result to see exactly what you're trading. Photos tolerate compression well; screenshots and flat graphics do better as PNG or at higher quality.

Which format should I pick?

AVIF is the smallest, roughly 20–30% below WebP at matching quality, and is supported by every current browser. WebP is the safe default with near-universal support. JPG for maximum compatibility with older software. PNG only when you need transparency or pixel-exact flat graphics.

Are my images uploaded anywhere?

No. Compression happens entirely inside your browser tab. Nothing is transmitted, stored, or logged, and there is no account to create.

Why did my PNG get bigger?

Re-encoding a PNG loses the original file's optimisation, so an already-optimised PNG can grow. Convert it to WebP or AVIF instead — that's where the real saving is. The tool flags this when it happens.

Is there a file size or count limit?

No. Very large images can be slow on phones since the work happens on your device, but there is no imposed cap on file size or how many you process.
